Taking Pictures in Space

The Cupola is an ESA-built observatory module of the International Space Station (ISS). Its seven windows are used to conduct experiments, dockings, and photograph Earth and space from the ISS.


Many of the most breathtaking images we have of other planets are taken by telescopes and orbiters. The James Webb Telescope that was launched on Christmas Day 2021 is the largest space telescope and orbits the Sun about 1 million miles from Earth to find the first galaxies that formed in the early universe using infrared imaging.
