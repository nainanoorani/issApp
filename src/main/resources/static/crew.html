A Day in the Life of an Astronaut

The ISS is a platform for long-term research for human health. Typically, the ISS hosts an international crew of 7 astronauts that live and work inside the ISS. This number can go up to 13 during changeover, and is opening up to private astronauts from companies like astronauts.

After the retirement of NASA's Space Shuttle in 2011, NASA astronauts typically travel to the space station via SpaceX's Crew Dragon capsule or the Russian Soyuz capsule. Crew Dragon began flying people starting with the Demo-2 mission that launched on May 30, 2020. Boeing's Starliner is preparing for launching humans after it's successful uncrewed Orbital Flight Test 2 (OFT-2) in 2022.

On station, astronauts spend about six months conducting various science experiments (have completed over 2500 to date) and maintaining and repairing the ISS. They also spend approximately two hours daily on exercise. They occasionally perform spacewalks to repair and install new hardware like the ROSA (Roll Out Solar Arrays) and Li-ion batteries to the space station.

NASA Record Holders: Mark Vande Hei (355 consecutive days in space), Peggy Whitson (665 cumulative days in space)
